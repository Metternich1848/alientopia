Ancient Mythologies of Snakes

A serpent tempted woman to eat the forbidden fruit, leading to the fall of man. Adam and Eve were banished from the Garden of Eden. The serpent was condemned to crawl on its belly. Since then, snakes have been associated with evil and sin.

Serpent in the Garden of Eden

Medusa was initially an attractive maiden. Following her assault by Poseidon, Athena punished Medusa by converting her beautiful hair into serpents and rendering her visage so terrible that anybody who gazed at her would be petrified.

Cursed Snake-haired Maiden

Yamata no Orochi is a brutal eight-headed serpentine dragon devouring a maiden each year. Susanoo, the storm deity, intoxicated the giant serpent with potent sake and then killed it while it was unconscious.

Mythical Eight-headed Serpent

Nüwa is a revered Chinese goddess who molded humans from Yellow River clay and repaired the shattered sky with five-colored stones. Her wisdom and courage saved humanity, symbolizing harmony and resilience.

Medical Use

Snake venom comprises numerous bioactive compounds that have been utilized to produce medications for the treatment of neurological disorders and cardiovascular diseases.

Culinary Offerings

Snake flesh is highly prized for its culinary excellence and nutritional content. For instance, snake soup and snake wine are two popular traditional cuisines in China.

Manufacturing Goods

Certain kinds of snakeskin are widely used in the manufacture of premium luxury items, including belts, boots, handbags, and wallets.
